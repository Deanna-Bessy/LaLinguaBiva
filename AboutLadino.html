About Ladino




"La lingua biva" means "the living language," and is an expsession used to describe the language of the Sephardic Jews, called Ladino or Djudeo-Espanyol. This website aims to work towards spreading knowledge and awareness of the language, keeping it alive by exposing new people to the language.
When the Sephardic Jews were expelled from Spain in 1492, they took 12th-century Castillian Spanish with them to their new homes. Continuing to speak their native language, the language began to pick up words and expressions from the new languages they were surrounded by, over hundreds of years becoming the language we know as Ladino. Various words in modern Ladino come originally from many diverse languages, including Hebrew, Arabic, Greek, French, and Turkish just to name a few.
While Ladino is no longer taught by parents to their children as a first language, there are still many Ladino speakers accross the world. These people write songs and poetry, teach others, give speaches, create books, and even write newspapers in Ladino to keep the language alive.
